This tool solves and plots the basic consumer problem often taught in undergraduate economics.

You may observe the plot of indifference curves and budget constraint subject to parameters you enter.

This tool assumes the consumer has a CES utility function, parameterized as

U ⁡ ( x , y ) = ( a ⁢ x ρ + ( 1 - a ) ⁢ y ρ ) 1 ρ

For ρ = 0 the limit of the CES utility function is the Cobb-Douglas function: U ⁡ ( x , y ) = x a ⁢ y 1 - a

The consumer chooses the quantities of x and y that maximize U ⁡ ( x , y ) subject to the budget constraint

p x ⁢ x + p x ⁢ y = m

where p x and p y are the prices of goods X and Y, x and y are the quantities purchased of each, and m is the consumer's income.

Be aware that the tool may be slow for certain combinations of parameters.
